Gain from Lectures on Street Vending Economy

Posted on 2021-02-08

​ Before launching the desk research on the street vending economy, I attended lectures given by Professor Huang of Sun Yat-sen University. It gave me a lot of inspiration and thought.

Reasons

​ First, we need to understand why we study on this topic today, which is related to the social and economic origin of the existence and development of urban street vendors.

The most common opinion is that most street vendors exist due to unemployment.

In many developing countries, economic reforms and underdevelopment, as well as unemployment caused by the financial crisis, have been cited as the main reasons for the remarkable expansion of vendors. (Cross 1998; Mitullah 2004;
Lonrenço-Lindell,2004; Skinner 2008;Chaudhuri 2009 )

​ However, this view and China’s actual situation is contradictory. Compared China with other developing countries, it is obvious that China has a relatively high GDP growth, and report shows that lack of needed labor happens in most places.

So here comes the problem, what is the socio-economic factors in the development of urban stallholders in China?

​ According to Pro. Huang ‘s research, farmers, workers, the unemployed, business people and salaried people are five types of people make up the street vendors in China.

​ Here is the main reasons, according to Pro. Huang.

  1. Overly exploitative working conditions drive workers (migrant workers) into hawkers
  2. Rural poverty drives farmers to the cities to work as street vendors, part-time or full-time
  3. Lack of job opportunities has encouraged the unemployed to seek their last livelihood as street vendors
  4. In an increasingly competitive business environment, businessmen or would-be businessmen become hawkers in order to seek transition, refuge or accumulation of capital
  5. Urban poverty encourages low-income workers to work part-time as street vendors to make up for their families

Problems

​ From the perspective of Chinese history, in different historical stages, national or local governments would adopt different attitudes and strategies to control street vendors due to different phased political goals

​ There are several reasons why we should ban urban street vendors now based on China’s national condition.

1. Hawkers clamp down on the implementation of development strategies embedded in the municipality to create a beautiful urban space and image and improve the capital attraction.
 2. Street vendors are chaotic symbols of space, which do not meet the national goal of a healthy city.
 3. There have been several clashes between street vendors and law enforcement groups in the city

Solutions

​ As can be seen from many cases in history, banning or expelling street vendors is neither inevitable nor unchangeable. It depends on how to define and view the position and significance of street vendors in social development, and how it relates to the political and economic goals of the government.

The conduct of conducts

​ Through the establishment of “mobile street vendors guidance area”, the guidance area reflects the spatial nature of governance, which realizes the discipline and transformation of behavior by placing mobile street vendors in a specific space.

Governmentality, generally referred to as the conduct of conducts, indicates generalized and diverse power-seeking practices aiming to form and guide the actions, behaviors and inner states of others or the self (Foucault,2007; Huxley, 2008)

‘The deliberations, strategies, tactics and devices employed by authorities for making up and acting upon a population and its constituents to ensure good and avert ill’. (Rose, 1996: 328)

The internal contradiction between the rationality and street vendors

Fixed location vs Mobility

​ Although fixed location management rebuilds the order of public space and facilitates management, it also damages the characteristics of mobility as the nature of mobile vendors

Rent payment vs Low cost

​ Low cost is the main characteristic of the mobile merchant economy, which not only makes it easy for the mobile merchant to enter, but also enables the practitioner to have a comparative competitive advantage compared with the regular business.

​ In terms of fairness, it is reasonable to pay rent because mobile vendors use public space resources and earn economic income from them.

​ However, rent collection has two negative consequences, which weaken the social justice value of the diversion area. Firstly, rent payment excludes street vendors with poor economic ability. Secondly, paying rent may would bring unsatisfied revenue to street vendors.

Contract constrain vs Flexibility

​ The system of lease contract used to restrain the main body of modern market economy stifles the flexibility characteristic of mobile traders, that is, it eliminates the irregular nature of mobile traders as a kind of employment that people can choose to enter and exit at any time according to actual needs, thus affecting the attraction of the distribution area

Product control vs Autonomy

`Product type regulations, while conducive to avoiding competition with formal commerce and maintaining the urban landscape, impair the autonomy of vendors to choose products according to market needs.

The artificial regulation of product types makes it impossible for vendors to choose products according to the surrounding market needs and consumer preferences.

Conclusion

There are still many problems for us to solve on the road of regulating and governing the stallholder economy.

My research topic would start from the view of economics, focused on the relationship between street vending economy and formal economy, and based on such basic desk research, if it is possible, I would like to conduct a social research to see the operation benefit of street vendors and its influencing factors in Nanjing, and to see how the condition of Covid-19 and changes in the regulation would affect it.

Thoughts on China's Encouragement of Street Vending Economy

Posted on 2021-02-05

Through reading relevant news and materials, I found that China had different attitudes towards the street vendor economy at different stages in history. Especially before the epidemic, China had a very strong resistance attitude towards the street vendor economy.

Then I have a question: How to understand the logic behind the behavior of China liberalized the regulation of the street vendors in the post-epidemic period?

Under the circumstance that the epidemic has been basically under control, China gradually loosened the control of street vendors. The reasons are all centered on the theme of restoring China’s economy and making all walks of life gradually get on the right track.

The main reason is to boost consumption as well as investment.

According to data, in the first 2 months of 2020, total retail sales of consumer goods reached 5.213 billion yuan, down 20.5 percent year on year. (National Bureau of Statistics)

The goods sold by street vendors are often cheaper than the same goods sold in stores, which greatly facilitates our consumption. At the same time, because of the epidemic, people are isolated at home, forbidden to go out. Many people very much want to go to some entertainment places to relax, and even some people will appear what we call revenge consumption behavior, so loosening the control of street vendors can stimulate the consumption of this part of the population. This is not only good for the country’s economy, but also to ease people’s tension during the epidemic, improve people’s quality of life during such period to some extent, and help maintain social stability.

While this could drive the operation of various industries, so as to help restore the normal operation of China’s economy and economic recovery.

In the first two months of 2020, the added value of industrial enterprises above designated size fell by 13.5% year-on-year, and the national service production index fell by 13.0% year-on-year. (National Bureau of Statistics)

By encouraging street vending to promote its related industries, such as the production of certain products, transportation and other aspects.

This also related to employment.

The unemployment rate has risen in January and February, particularly in February, when it hit 6.2 percent, compared with 5.3 percent in January. (National Bureau of Statistics)

The main reason for the rise in the unemployment rate in February was still the impact of the epidemic, which affected the production and business activities of enterprises and reduced the demand for employment, thus reducing the number of employed people. With a low threshold, everyone can operate the business, which can greatly increase the number of jobs, especially for the people who cannot find suitable jobs. The street vending economy can help them create profits, increase income and improve the life of the whole family. With the recovery of the production order of enterprises driven by it, the employment rate of enterprises is constantly improving.

In general, liberalizing the regulation of the market economy will not only help promote China’s economic recovery, but also enrich people’s lives and maintain social stability. Although there are some risks, such as food safety and epidemic prevention and control, these risks can be avoided under certain control, so that China’s various industries can get on the right track.

Perspectives on the significance of the existence and development of street vendors

Posted on 2021-02-03

[TOC]

For vendors

The development of urban street vendors can improve urban happiness index and the standard of living of some people to a certain extent. Based on Pro. Huang, structural development of urban street vendors in China and to the exploration of the conclusion, we learn that due to the reconstruction of capitalism that an employment instability and exploitation as well as rural or urban new relative poverty causes the phenomenon of a rapidly growing number of city street vendors, the development of the urban street vendors help to make this kind of people have some disposable income, alleviate the pressure of the life. In order to ensure the quality of life of this part of the population.

To consumers

Vendors offer consumers a great deal of convenience. Such a format allows sellers to connect with buyers quickly and, in general, the goods offered by vendors are characterized by low prices. These characteristics enable consumers to enjoy cheaper，faster and customized consumption and services.

To city

For China, the existence of street vendors has existed for a long time. Under the premise of certain historical significance, the existence of urban street vendors also represents the local culture and characteristics.

In many cities, the gathering places of city vendors have also become the local cultural tourism label, which is one of the reasons to attract tourists. Such a cultural label of a city will not only improve the happiness of city residents, but also attract more people to enter the city to some extent, which would improve the economic development of the city to some extent.

To economy

Those mentioned above encouraging expenditure and investment, regarded as a big source of local GDP.

Street vending also contributes to the local employment rate, as it not only creates jobs for vendors, but also for various others such as porters, farmer, transport operators, etc.

Many revenues could be generated through payment for licenses and permits, fees and fines, and other certain taxes.

Street vendors have a strong linkage to the formal economy. They source the goods they sell from formal enterprises, and many customers, even some of the vendors, work in formal jobs.

In a sense, it could also balance the income distribution of local economy.

Especially during the period of Covid-19, street vending has significant importance ever. By using street vending as a way of stimulating the economy, China is supporting small business and improving consumer confidence, thus recovery the economy.

Desk Research Methods

Posted on 2021-02-03

[TOC]

​

​ Due to the impact of Covid-19 this year, there are certain restrictions on the way we conduct research, so many people use desktop research methods for academic research.

What is desk research?

Desk research involves reanalysis, interpretation or review past data. The role of the researcher is always to specify how past data informs his or her current research. Compared with first-hand research, desktop research is easier, especially because researchers are less involved in the actual data collection process.

Steps

​ Since this holiday I will carry out a desktop study on the street vendor economy, the following step-by-step instructions for the desk study also initially show the general steps and logic of my research, so I will also use the street vendor economy as an example to illustrate

Step 1 Present your research questions

​ First, determine the general topic, and then read through the existing papers to find out whether the direction I determine can fill the gaps in the existing academic research, and on this basis, determine the specific research direction.

Step 2 Find secondary data sets

​ Most research begins by specifying what is known and what seems to be missing on the subject. This process involves considering the types of data previously collected for the topic. At this point, after reviewing the literature and specifying the research questions, a decision can be made rely on secondary data. You can do this if you find that past data can be fully reused in your own research.

​ But how can you find past data useful for your research? You can do this by looking at literature related to topics of interest to you. In this process, you will identify other researchers, organizations, institutions, or research centers that have explored your research topic.

​ Most importantly, be sure to get the permission of the original author before using the data.

Step 3 Evaluate secondary data sets

​ Because the data we collected may have some defects, such as lack of reliability or not consistent with our research topic, we need to evaluate these collected data and data sets.

​ You could ask yourself several questions to conduct this process, for example:

  1. What is the purpose of original research, whether it match your chosen topic?

     2.  Who, when, and how did the data collected, whether it is reliable?

Step 4 Prepare and analyze auxiliary data

After going through all those process above, you need to start preparing and analyzing. This depends on whether the research is qualitative or quantitative. Personally, I would conduct the street vendor economy research qualitatively first, after that, I would start to collect my primary data based on the qualitative outcome, and then start the quantitative research.
