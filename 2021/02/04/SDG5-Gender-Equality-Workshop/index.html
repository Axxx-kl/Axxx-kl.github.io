SDG5 Gender Equality Workshop

Posted on 2021-02-04

This is the first time that I participated in a workshop on the sustainable development of the United Nations. The theme of this workshop is about gender equality, which is also one of the sustainable development goals that I personally pay more attention to.

I learned a lot of professional knowledge about gender equality, as well as some information and expectations of the United Nations for this sustainable goal. For example, data shows that women did most of the housework and unpaid work, and whatâ€™s interesting is that phones and internet could promote the development gender equality. However, there was big disaster in 2020, which was Covid-19. It is shocked that Covid-19 push development of gender equality back to 25 years ago, which is really unbelievable. We could see that Covid-19 had a numerous effect not only on economy and health care, but also on humanity.

One big part what I extremely focused on is domestic abuse, which is also a big topic under gender equality. I learnt a project that help overseas Chinese with domestic abuse. This is also the first project in China that pays attention to this aspect.
These overseas cases are more difficult to deal with, mainly due to the identity restrictions of these Chinese, language barriers and cultural conflicts. One case I learnt made me think about not only on gender equality, but also on human equal rights and racisms, because the Chinese women in this case was discriminated by her lawyer as well as her jury which cause her to lose the case.

During the discussion time, I found that most cases we talk about are about females. It seems like gender equality is only about help female get their equal rights, which to some extent, is not exactly the same. Though at the first beginning, the start of gender equality was about female indeed, but as it developing, it is more about bring both female and male to a same social stage. We do need to help those female who are in trouble, because most of them are struggling in the dis-equality, however, at the same time, we should also pay attention to male, which is important as well.

In my opinion, the first step we need to do is to break those stereotypes. I talked to many people in the workshop, some of them are founders of social enterprises and some of them are doing such work to promote the development of gender equality. Then I found that what they did was really encouraging, which is what I want to do in the future, and I get more and more interested in such work. I plan to join more in such activities in the future to have the chance to communicate with different people from different background, and I really want to be one of the members of them.
